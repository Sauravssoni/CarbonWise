**Draft Copy:**

🌍 How do our daily routines compound into our global footprint? 

Most carbon calculators are either too complicated or rely on broad averages that don't help us build concrete habits. That's why I built **CarbonWise**—a high-fidelity, custom-built carbon impact tracking application with an interactive footprint wizard, dynamic visual analytics, and live AI integration!

Designed using a meticulous, slate-and-emerald minimalist theme, **CarbonWise** guides you through:
1. 🚗 **Transportation & Commutes** — Analyzing fuel outputs and carpool benefits.
2. ⚡ **Home Utilities** — Scaling your monthly direct kWh or bill estimates partitioned by household occupancy.
3. 🥗 **Dietary Choices** — Calculating different dietary impacts, food waste frequency, and restaurant packaging footprints.
4. 🛍️ **Apparel & Deliveries** — Estimating the pre-consumer carbon watermarks of e-commerce packaging and fashion shipments.
5. 🎯 **Carbon Reduction Goals** — Defining active targets and commitment levels.

### 🧠 The Tech Underneath:
* **Custom Full-Stack Server** — An Express.ts backend bound natively with Vite.
* **Deterministic Fuel Calculation Engine** — Developed with transparent reference benchmarks (DEFRA 2023, IEA Emission Factors v1.4) ensuring mathematically stable baselines.
* **Smart Gemini AI Integration** — When enabled, the server leverages **Gemini 3.5 Flash** behind secure `/api/*` proxies to generate fully personalized, contextual insights and motivational nudges based on user-entered targets—falling back gracefully to the deterministic model on network breaks.
* **Local Persistence Loop** — Maintains offline-first privacy by caching results and checkins inside local storage, rendering live timelines, progress metrics, and action commitments.
* **Flawless UI & Micro-animations** — Fully accessible keyboard navigation, gorgeous charts powered by SVG, and elegant touch target padding.

Tackling carbon emissions doesn't start with overwhelming guilt—it starts with **precise awareness**. 100% of our test suites (32 unit tests) pass with flying colors and zero linter warnings.
